Nutrition Facts

Fats

Tuna is high in omega-3 fatty acids but low in overall fat, with "light" tuna cans containing less than 2 grams of fat each. However, fat content varies across different types of tuna. The ranking of common varieties based on fat content, from highest to lowest, is as follows: fresh bluefin, canned white albacore tuna, canned light tuna, fresh skipjack tuna, and fresh yellowfin tuna.

Protein

Tuna is an excellent source of protein, offering 42 grams per can, providing all essential amino acids.

Vitamins and Minerals

Tuna is rich in various essential nutrients such as calcium, phosphorus, potassium, zinc, B-vitamins, selenium, and choline. It's important to note that like other canned foods, tuna can be high in sodium. Consumers should check food labels to identify low-sodium options or products with no added salt.

Carbs

Tuna contains negligible amounts of carbohydrates, fiber, or sugar.
